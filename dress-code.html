Home/Dress Code

Dress code.

What to wear to class — adults, women, and kids. The first class only requires comfortable shorts and a clean T-shirt; the rest is once you commit.

First Class

Shorts.
A clean shirt.

For your free trial class, you don't need to buy anything. Just show up in comfortable shorts (no zippers or rivets) and a clean T-shirt or rash guard. We've got the rest.

A1

Adult uniform.

For adult classes you'll wear a clean T-shirt or rash guard with shorts. Grappling shorts (no pockets) are preferred. Jean shorts and shorts with rivets are not permitted. Sleeveless T's and crop tops aren't permitted.

A2

Rash guards & shorts.

Once you decide to keep training, a couple of rash guards and grappling shorts will last you for years. We have some recommendations for you linked below.

W1

Women's uniform.

The same as men's. If you prefer, you may wear leggings or spats instead of shorts. Women's-cut rash guards are widely available — just ask.

K1

Kids' uniform.

Plain white Gi for Gi class. Plain rash guard + shorts for No-Gi. Use the brand size charts to fit your child — Gis run a little long. We'll adjust if it's off.

K2

Kids gear pieces.

Kids Gi (white). Kids rash guard. Kids shorts. We sell Mooresville Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu-branded kids gear at the front desk if you want the team look.

H1

Hygiene.

Wash all training clothes after every class. Shower before and after. Keep your nails short — long nails cause injury and infection during grappling. We clean the mats daily without exception.

Hygiene

Keep your gear fresh.

Properly cleaning your training gear is essential to maintain hygiene and prevent unpleasant odors. Here are some tips to ensure your gear stays fresh and clean:

  1. Clean immediately after class. It's important to wash your training gear promptly after each session. Leaving sweaty gear in your gym bag can lead to the growth of bacteria and unpleasant odors.
  2. Use soap and Odoban. When washing your gear, use a quality detergent along with a splash of Odoban. Odoban is a disinfectant and deodorizer that helps eliminate odors and kill bacteria. Follow the instructions on the Odoban bottle for the appropriate amount to use.
  3. Machine wash or hand wash. Depending on the fabric and care instructions, you can either machine wash or hand wash your gear. Check the labels or consult the manufacturer's guidelines to ensure you're using the correct washing method.
  4. Hang dry or use a dryer. After washing, you can either hang your gear to dry or use a dryer if the fabric allows it. Hang drying helps prevent shrinkage and extends the lifespan of your gear. If using a dryer, use a low heat setting to avoid damaging the fabric.
  5. Regularly clean accessories. Don't forget to clean other accessories like mouthguards, headgear, and protective gear. Follow the manufacturer's instructions for cleaning these items, as they may require specific care.

By following these cleaning practices, you can keep your training gear fresh, hygienic, and ready for your next session. Remember, maintaining clean gear not only benefits your own hygiene but also shows respect for your training partners.
